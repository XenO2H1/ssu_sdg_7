What is SDG 7?

Sustainable Development Goal 7 is a universal call to action to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all by 2030. It's a cornerstone for eradicating poverty, fighting climate change, and building prosperous, resilient communities.

One Goal, Many Connections

Achieving SDG 7 is fundamental to the success of many other Sustainable Development Goals.

No Poverty (SDG 1)

Energy access powers economic opportunities for the poor.

Good Health (SDG 3)

Clean energy reduces pollution and powers healthcare facilities.

Sustainable Cities (SDG 11)

Powers sustainable transport and urban infrastructure.

Climate Action (SDG 13)

Shifting to renewables is key to combating climate change.

Why Clean Energy Matters

Transitioning to clean energy creates a ripple effect of positive change.

Environmental Health

Reduces air and water pollution, combats climate change by cutting greenhouse gas emissions, and protects biodiversity.

Economic Growth

Creates green jobs in manufacturing and installation, stimulates innovation, and provides energy security by reducing reliance on imported fuels.

Social Well-being

Improves public health with cleaner air, enhances energy access for rural communities, and supports education and healthcare services.

Types of Clean Energy

Solar Power

Harnessing sunlight via photovoltaic panels, solar is a fast-growing, clean, and abundant source for generating electricity.

Wind Power

Wind turbines convert the kinetic energy of wind into electricity. It's a non-polluting and sustainable source of power.

Hydropower

Hydropower uses the force of flowing water to spin turbines, providing a stable and reliable source of clean electricity.

Geothermal

This energy source taps into the Earth's internal heat to generate steam, which powers electricity generators.

Biomass

Biomass energy is generated from organic materials like wood, crops, and waste, turning them into a renewable power source.

Tidal Energy

A form of hydropower that converts the. energy obtained from tides into useful forms of power, mainly electricity.

India's Progress Towards SDG 7

India is making significant strides in its clean energy transition, becoming a global leader in renewables.

500 GW

India's ambitious goal for non-fossil fuel energy capacity by 2030, a cornerstone of its climate commitment.

190+ GW

Total installed renewable energy capacity, making India one of the world's largest clean energy producers.

99%+

Households electrified under national schemes, achieving near-universal energy access for millions.

Be a Part of the Solution

Individual actions, when combined, create a powerful movement for change. Here’s how you can contribute to India's clean energy goals.

1. Conserve Energy

Reduce your electricity consumption by using energy-efficient appliances, switching off lights, and being mindful of your daily usage.

2. Adopt Renewables

Consider installing rooftop solar panels. Many government subsidies and schemes make this more affordable than ever.

3. Support Green Policies

Stay informed about and support local and national policies that promote renewable energy and energy efficiency standards.

4. Educate & Advocate

Spread awareness in your community about the importance of clean energy and encourage others to take action.
